Cyber Safety and Security

What is Cyber Safety?

Cyber Safety refers to the safe and responsible use of internet to ensure personal protection and avoid cybercrimes. In today's digital world, students must be aware of the dangers of the internet and how to stay safe.

Types of Cyber Threats

  • Phishing: Fake emails or messages that steal personal information.
  • Hacking: Unauthorized access to computer systems or data.
  • Cyberbullying: Online harassment or bullying on social media platforms.
  • Malware: Viruses or harmful software that can damage devices.
  • Identity Theft: Stealing someone's personal data and pretending to be them.

Why is Cyber Safety Important?

Cyber safety protects your identity, data, and mental health. It helps students to avoid scams, bullying, and online traps that can cause serious damage.

Tips to Stay Safe Online

  • Use strong passwords and change them regularly.
  • Do not share personal details like address, school, phone number online.
  • Think before posting anything on social media.
  • Install antivirus and update your software regularly.
  • Always log out from shared devices.

Important Cyber Laws in India

  • Information Technology Act, 2000
  • Cyber Crime Cells under local police departments
  • Punishment for identity theft and hacking

Real-Life Example

In 2023, a student shared personal information on a gaming site and became a victim of fraud. This shows the importance of being alert and aware while using the internet.

How Students Can Help Others

  • Report cyber crimes to parents or teachers.
  • Raise awareness in your class or school.
  • Support friends who face online bullying.
